Panchakosha is a yoga idea about the five coverings of people. It looks at different parts or levels of human beings in philosophy. These coverings are believed to include different parts of a person, making an overall view about life.

Pancha Kosha Yoga

The Panchakosha model comes from old yogic writings like the Taittiriya Upanishad and the Taittiriya Aranyaka. These ancient texts are where this idea started. Let’s explore each of the Panchakoshas:

Annamaya Kosha

The physical body or the food sheath.
It represents the physical aspect of the human being.

This outside part is about the body you can see, such as bones, muscles and organs. It also includes things that make up your whole human shape. Practices linked to Annamaya Kosha involve doing yoga poses (asanas) and eating mindfully to keep our body healthy.

Pranamaya Kosha

The energy or life-force sheath.
It governs the flow of vital energy in the body through breath and prana.

Pranamaya Kosha is about the vital life energy that makes our body come alive. It includes air, blood flow and energy all over the body. Breath control exercises called pranayama are very important for keeping the Pranamaya Kosha in balance.

Manomaya Kosha

The mental or emotional sheath.
It involves thoughts, emotions, and mental processes.

Manomaya Kosha is linked with the mind and feelings of a person. It includes feelings, emotions and how the mind works. Activities such as meditating, being mindful and saying good things try to help the mental health of Manomaya Kosha.

Vijnanamaya Kosha

The wisdom sheath. It represents the intellect and the higher discriminative mind.

This sheath shows smart thinking, good judgment and making smart decisions. Vijnanamaya Kosha is linked to intuition, seeing more deeply and understanding oneself better. Practices that help us understand ourselves, ask questions about life and learn new things build up our Vijnanamaya Kosha.

Anandamaya Kosha

The bliss sheath.
It is the innermost layer, associated with deep peace and joy.

The deepest part is Anandamaya Kosha, connected to the happiness and joy that goes beyond pleasure and pain. It symbolizes a deep calm and happiness. Praying, being loyal and touching one’s inner feeling help in Anandamaya Kosha enjoyment.
